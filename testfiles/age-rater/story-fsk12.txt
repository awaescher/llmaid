The Shadow Protocol - Chapter 7

Agent Marcus Cole crouched behind the crumbling concrete wall, his heart pounding in his chest. The enemy soldiers were getting closer—he could hear their boots crunching on the gravel path.

"Damn it," he muttered under his breath, checking his ammunition. Only three rounds left. This mission had gone sideways fast.

His contact, a woman known only as Raven, had betrayed him. She'd sold out the entire operation to the highest bidder, leaving Marcus to clean up the mess. He'd trusted her, and that mistake might cost him his life.

A bullet whizzed past his head, chipping away at the wall. Marcus rolled to the side, returning fire. One soldier dropped. Two more took his place.

"Come out, Agent Cole!" a voice called out. "Make this easy on yourself!"

Marcus grinned despite the situation. Easy wasn't in his vocabulary. He'd been in worse situations before—back in Prague, in that warehouse full of armed mercenaries. He'd survived that. He'd survive this too.

He pulled a smoke grenade from his belt and hurled it over the wall. Thick gray smoke filled the courtyard, giving him the cover he needed. Moving quickly, he slipped through the shadows toward the extraction point.

His phone buzzed. A message from headquarters: "Helicopter en route. ETA 5 minutes. Hold tight."

Five minutes. He could do this. He had to.

The Shadow Protocol had to be stopped, no matter the cost. Too many innocent lives were at stake. Marcus had seen what this weapon could do, and he wouldn't let it fall into the wrong hands.

He took a deep breath, steadied his aim, and prepared for the final confrontation.
